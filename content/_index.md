There’s a thought a lot of capable people carry: *there’s something more I should be doing, but I don’t know what it is.* So the searching becomes the avoidance — busy with the seeking, exhausted by the breadth, doing everything except the one thing.

<!--col2-->

The calling was never lost. It’s been patient — waiting while you ran around answering everyone else. You’re not building something new. You’re retrieving something that was always yours.

The work you were hiding behind often mattered enormously — caring for, feeding, teaching. That’s exactly why it made such a good hiding place. You can matter a great deal to other people and still be in quiet avoidance of the thing that is specifically yours.
